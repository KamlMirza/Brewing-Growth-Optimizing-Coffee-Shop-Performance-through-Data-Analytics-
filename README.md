# Coffee Shop Sales Analysis Dashboard

## Project Objective

The primary goal of this project was to transform raw retail transactional data into an interactive Excel dashboard that provides actionable business intelligence. By analyzing sales patterns, customer behavior, and product performance, the project identifies key revenue drivers and delivers data-backed recommendations to optimize operational efficiency and profitability.

## Approach

To ensure a structured and accurate analysis, the following methodology was used:

### 1. Data Cleaning and Preparation
- Standardized date and time formats.
- Handled missing and inconsistent values.
- Created helper columns such as **Month**, **Day of Week**, and **Hour** to support time-series analysis.

### 2. Data Modeling
- Used **Pivot Tables** to aggregate and summarize large datasets.
- Calculated key performance indicators:
  - Total Revenue
  - Transaction Count
  - Average Order Value (AOV)

### 3. Analysis Dimensions

#### Temporal Analysis
- Monthly revenue trends
- Day-of-week performance
- Hourly sales distribution

#### Geographic Analysis
- Performance comparison across store locations:
  - Hell’s Kitchen
  - Astoria
  - Lower Manhattan

#### Product Analysis
- Top-selling categories by revenue
- Best-performing products by volume
- Contribution of add-on items to total bill value

### 4. Data Visualization
- Clustered Column Charts for revenue comparison
- Line Charts for growth trends
- Heatmaps for peak-hour identification
- Interactive slicers for dynamic filtering

## Results and Key Insights

The analysis revealed the following important patterns:

### Steady Growth Trend
Sales demonstrated consistent month-over-month growth, reaching a peak revenue of **$166.5K in June**.

### Peak Sales Window
The highest revenue concentration occurs between **8:00 AM and 10:00 AM**, serving as the primary driver of daily transactions.

### Weekly Distribution
Revenue remains relatively stable throughout the week, with **Thursdays and Fridays** performing slightly stronger than other days.

### Location Performance
- Hell’s Kitchen and Astoria consistently generate higher revenue.
- Lower Manhattan shows comparatively moderate performance.

### Revenue Drivers
- **Coffee and Tea** contribute the largest share of total revenue.
- **Bakery items** significantly increase Average Order Value when purchased alongside beverages.

## Strategic Recommendations

Based on the findings, the following actions are recommended:

### 1. Maximize Peak Hours
Introduce bundled promotions (e.g., Coffee + Pastry discounts) between 8:00 AM and 10:00 AM to increase Average Order Value.

### 2. Improve Afternoon Sales
Launch targeted promotions between 2:00 PM and 4:00 PM to address mid-day sales slowdowns.

### 3. Optimize Operations
Adjust staffing and inventory levels, particularly on Fridays, to efficiently manage higher transaction volumes.

### 4. Controlled Product Testing
Use Hell’s Kitchen and Astoria as pilot locations for launching premium or seasonal products before expanding to other branches.
